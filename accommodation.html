You Are Guaranteed

Undergrads, Postgrads, and Visitng Students alike, the University provides you with a guarantee of accommodation during your time here at the University of Edinburgh.

Undergrads are guaranteed if...

  • You are a new, single student to the University
  • You submit your accommodation application by the 16th of August in your year of entry
  • Your UCAS considers you UF (Unconditional Firm) by the 30th of August in you year of entry
  • You live outside of the city of Edinburgh
  • You start studying at the University of Edinburgh for a whole academic year starting in September

For your Undergrad accommodation guidebook click here

Postgrads are guaranteed if...

  • You are a new, single postgrad research (PhD) from any part of the world
  • OR You are a new, single, postgraduate taught student from outside the EU
  • You submit your accommodation application by the 31st of July in your year of entry
  • You accepted you unconditonal offer to study at the University by the 31st of July
  • You will be studying for the entire academic year at Edinburgh starting September

For your Postgrad accommodation guidebook click here

Visiting students are guaranteed if...

  • You submit your accommodation application by the 16th of August in your year of entry
  • You accept your unconditional offer to study at the University by the 30th of August
  • You are applying outside the EU
  • You are studying at the University for the entire academic year
  • You are a non-EU visitng undergrad studying for a single semester

For your visitng undergrad accommodation guidebook click here

FAQs

When should I apply for accommodation?

Whenever you accept your unconditional offer to study is the best time apply, as the application immediately opens on your MyEd Accommodation channel when you do accept. It is in your best interest to apply as soon as possible to get the accommodation you most desire!

When will I find out about my offer for accommodation?

Offers of accommodation will go out from June onwards to those with Unconditional offers to study at the University. Those waiting on exam results will be sent offers once your exam results have been received by the University.

What should I do if I am unable to apply online?

If for some reason you are unable to apply online, please call Allocations on +44 (0) 131 651 2042.

FAQs

What is the difference between self-catererd and catered?

Self-catered flats provide a kitchen for the student to use. Students are responsible for cooking their own meals and budgeting accordingly. Catered flats provide the student a meal plan to where the university provides you meals via Pollock Halls. More information can be found here about catered halls.

How does accommodation selection work?

Students are asked to rank their accommodations according to their preferences. Every student thus has the same chance at receving whatever accommodation they applied for. University accommodation is not done in a first come, first served basis

What to expect

You will be taken care of

Being a new at University is hard enough, learning how to live independantly is even harder! Every accommodation is equipped with professional staff and students to help you better adjust to living here at the University.

Your accommodation team comes with...

Wardens
Wardens are the head administrator of your accommodation, you will find that they will help you with any logistical support as well as maintenance and your overall wellbeing at your time here!

Resident Assistances (RAs)
-You can count on RAs for administrative support much like Wardens will, but RAs are designed to help you get accustomed to University life on a personal level as well. They are here to help foster a socially inclusive environment for you as well as give you opportunities to meet new people and build friendships.

Events are a great way to connect!

Events are what the Residential Assistants use to create opportunities to see the city and meet new poeple. Every student's accommodation is equipped with their own events that their RAs plan out and advertise, but a more general calendar for the bigger events can be found here!

FAQs

How do I contact my RA before moving in?

When you accept your accommodation, you will be emailed a link to a group page, usually through Facebook where you can contact your RAs there. If you are immediately concerned, please seek out your accommodation's contact info in the Contact Us section.
What should I do for arrival?

Visit your MyEd approximately 2 weeks prior to the move in date on September 10th and complete the arrival information section like this in your MyEd Accommodation channel.

Process

No Guarantee? We can help

If you are not guaranteed university accommodation or are seeking the private sector, we are here to help! The univesity provides resources for helping you find available housing. Be aware that this process varies from landlord to landlord, but we act as a liason in your support!

Some Helpful Tips

Don't be a victim!
Be mindful of any adverts for private accommodation around the city. Never send money before viewing the accommodation and before seeing that the landlord or letting agency is legimiate and registered. To find out if the agency is legitimate, please check these websites. Keep in mind that the letter's membership is voluntary under these websites.

Association of Rental Letting Agents
National Approved Letting Scheme
The Property Ombudsman

Know your renting obligations
Signing a lease is a legally binding contract that needs to be understood and taken seriously. Do not be ill prepared and take some time to understand your rights!

We provide many resources at your service

The renting process is an involved process that is very difficult to do on your own, especially if you are an international student. We provide support to you at your request through the many teams at the university.
